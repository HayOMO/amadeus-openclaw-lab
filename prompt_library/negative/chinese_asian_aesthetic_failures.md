Use only as a secondary negative card when the user explicitly wants a
Chinese/Asian social-media look, anime identity preservation, or asks to avoid
westernized / generic-oriental defaults. Do not use as a positive style recipe
and do not attach it to ordinary portraits, generic anime scenes, or fashion
moodboards by default.

Avoid:

- Western influencer glam when the user asks for 小红书/日系/韩系/国风.
- Eurocentric facial reshaping, heavy contour, plastic skin, over-tanned palette.
- Generic "oriental" props, random dragons/lanterns/calligraphy, fake temple
  backgrounds.
- Beige-only lifestyle palette with no hierarchy.
- Overloaded stickers, fake app UI clutter, unreadable dense Chinese text.
- Anime identity drift: wrong hair/eye color, random outfit, western fantasy
  costume when original/canonical design is requested.
- Hanfu dynasty mashups when historical precision matters.

Use narrow negatives. Do not paste this whole list into every generation; pick
the relevant 2-4 failure modes.
